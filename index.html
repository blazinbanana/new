Intensive Care Unit (ICU) — Central System Node
Continuous critical care · Multidisciplinary team · Highest technology dependency
CENTRAL NODE
Inputs
Critically ill patients (A&E, theatre, wards) Intensivists, ICU nurses (1:2 ratio) Clinical officers, physiotherapists IV drugs: vasopressors, sedatives, antibiotics Blood products & IV fluids Uninterrupted O₂ & power (UPS) Referral notes, lab results, imaging Enteral / parenteral nutrition
Core Processes
Admission triage — APACHE/SOFA scoring Mechanical ventilation (invasive & NIV) Haemodynamic monitoring & resuscitation Sedation, analgesia & pain scoring (RASS) Medication administration (IV infusions) Renal replacement therapy (dialysis) Infection control — VAP / CLABSI bundles Daily MDT ward rounds Family communication & consent Nutrition assessment & delivery Physiotherapy / mobility Step-down & discharge planning
Outputs
Stabilised patient → HDU / ward Extubated patient (ventilator wean) Post-op recovery complete Clinical data → QI / audit Teaching & research output NHIF / SHA billing records In-hospital mortality Healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) Unplanned readmission Transfer out (capacity exceeded)

Laboratory
Biochemistry, haematology, microbiology, histopathology
Inputs
Blood, urine, CSF specimens Surgical tissue (from theatre) Lab scientists & technicians Analysers, reagents, culture media LIS (Lab Information System) Refrigeration & cold chain
Processes
Full blood count (FBC) U&E, LFTs, coagulation Arterial blood gas (ABG/POCT) Blood cultures (sepsis protocol) Sensitivity & susceptibility Cross-match & blood grouping Histopathology & cytology
Outputs
Results → LIS → HIS → clinician Sensitivity report → antibiotic guide Histology report → oncology / surgery TAT bottleneck: 4–6h resource-limited Reagent stockout = no results Night shift: 1–2 technicians for all tests
Technology Dependencies — Critical Equipment Across All Departments
Mechanical ventilators (ICU) Multi-parameter bedside monitors Infusion pumps & syringe drivers Defibrillator / AED ABG analyser (POCT) Portable ultrasound (POCUS) CT scanner MRI machine Portable X-ray unit C-arm / fluoroscopy (theatre) Anaesthetic machine Surgical diathermy / electrocautery CSSD autoclave / steriliser Lab auto-analysers (haem, biochem) Blood culture system (BACTEC) HIS — Hospital Information System LIS — Laboratory Information System PACS — Picture Archiving & Communication Piped O₂ / concentrator / bulk LO₂ UPS & diesel generator backup Pneumatic tube system (specimens) Telemedicine / teleconsult platform
System Bottlenecks & Risk Points — Where Flow Breaks Down
Power outages → ventilator risk (ICU) O₂ supply gaps → cylinder dependency Intensivist shortage — <100 nationally (Kenya) Brain drain: nurses & doctors emigrating Equipment breakdown — long procurement cycle ICU bed scarcity: 0.1–0.3 per 10,000 population Blood bank stockouts — irregular KNBTS supply Lab TAT delays — night shift understaffing CSSD failure = cancelled elective theatre Shared anaesthetist pool — ICU vs theatre conflict No integrated HIS in county hospitals NHIF/SHA reimbursement delays → cash-flow strain Reagent stockouts → no lab results Portable X-ray unavailable → ICU imaging delayed Patient transport instability → CT scan risk
